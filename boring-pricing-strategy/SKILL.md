## World Code Pricing Principles

### Value Metric = Transformation Delivered
Your value metric should scale with how much of the Climax transformation the customer receives. More transformation = higher price.

### Method Phases = Tier Structure
Map pricing tiers to Method phases:
- **Free/Entry**: Method Phase 1 (enough to prove the approach works)
- **Core**: Full Method (the complete transformation path)
- **Premium**: Method + advanced support + community (accelerated transformation)

### BONES = Value Justification
Use the BONES breakdown from `creation.md` to justify pricing:
- **Bonuses**: What extras do they get?
- **Outcomes**: What transformation results?
- **Necessities**: What essential elements are included?
- **Experience**: What's the delivery experience?
- **Stack**: What's the total value stack?

### Price Anchoring Against the Before State Cost
What does staying in the Before State cost them? (time, money, frustration, missed opportunities)
Your price should feel small compared to the cost of NOT transforming.

## Pricing Fundamentals

### Value-Based Pricing
- **Customer's perceived transformation value** — The ceiling
- **Your price** — Between alternatives and perceived value
- **Next best alternative** — The floor (What does the Method's "Why It Works Differently" buy you?)
- **Your cost to serve** — Only a baseline

### Tier Structure (Method-Mapped)

| Tier | Method Access | Transformation Level | Pricing |
|------|--------------|---------------------|---------|
| Free/Entry | Phase 1 only | Taste of transformation | Free or low |
| Core | All phases | Full transformation | From `creation.md` |
| Premium | All phases + acceleration | Faster/deeper transformation | 2-3x Core |
