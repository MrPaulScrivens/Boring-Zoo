## Core Principles

### 1. Honesty Builds Trust
Acknowledge competitor strengths. Be accurate about your limitations. Readers are comparing — they'll verify claims.

### 2. Depth Over Surface
Go beyond feature checklists. Explain *why* differences matter through your Method lens.

### 3. Help Them Decide
Be clear about who you're best for (Climax audience) and who competitor is best for.

### 4. Frame Through Your World Code
Every comparison should ultimately show why your Method, Climax promise, and Creation are the right choice for your specific audience — not for everyone.

## Page Formats

### Format 1: [Competitor] Alternative (Singular)

**URL pattern**: `/alternatives/[competitor]`
**Target**: Users actively looking to switch

**World Code page structure:**
1. Why people look for alternatives (validate their pain using Climax Before State)
2. Your approach (introduce your Method as the alternative)
3. Detailed comparison (frame through Method differentiation)
4. Who should switch (your Climax audience) and who shouldn't
5. The transformation they can expect (Climax promise)
6. Social proof from switchers
7. CTA (Crossing entry point)

### Format 2: [Competitor] Alternatives (Plural)

**URL pattern**: `/alternatives/[competitor]-alternatives`
**Target**: Users researching options

**World Code structure:**
1. Why people look for alternatives (Climax Before State pain)
2. What to look for (criteria framed by your Method principles)
3. List of alternatives (you first, include real options)
4. Comparison table
5. Recommendation by use case (your Climax audience = your recommendation)
6. CTA

### Format 3: You vs [Competitor]

**URL pattern**: `/vs/[competitor]`
**Target**: Direct comparison shoppers

**World Code structure:**
1. TL;DR (Method differentiator in 2-3 sentences)
2. At-a-glance comparison table
3. Detailed comparison by category (framed through World Code)
4. Who you're best for (Climax audience, honestly)
5. Who competitor is best for (honesty builds trust)
6. Transformation stories from people who chose you
7. Migration support
8. CTA (Crossing entry point)

### Format 4: [Competitor A] vs [Competitor B]

**URL pattern**: `/compare/[competitor-a]-vs-[competitor-b]`
**Target**: Competitor traffic capture

**Structure:**
1. Overview of both products
2. Comparison by category
3. Who each is best for
4. The third option — introduce yourself through your Method
5. Three-way comparison table
6. CTA

## Essential Sections

### TL;DR Summary
Lead with your Method differentiator. What makes your approach fundamentally different?

### Comparison Tables
Frame categories around what matters to your Climax audience, not generic feature lists.

### "Who It's For" Section
Be specific using your Climax language:
- "Choose [You] if you [Climax Before State characteristics]"
- "Choose [Competitor] if you [different needs]"

### Migration Section
Cover what changes, support offered, and include testimonials from people who made the switch and experienced the Climax transformation.
