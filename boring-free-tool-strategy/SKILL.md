## Core Principles

### 1. Solve a Real Problem
The tool must provide genuine value to your Climax audience.

### 2. Adjacent to Core Offer
The tool should naturally lead people toward your Creation. The best free tools give users a taste of one Method phase.

### 3. Simple and Focused
Does one thing well. Low friction. Immediate value.

### 4. Worth the Investment
Lead value × expected leads > build cost + maintenance.

## The Tool-to-Offer Bridge

The free tool should naturally bridge to your Creation:

```
Free Tool (taste of Method Phase 1)
  → User sees their Before State clearly
    → Wants the full transformation (Climax promise)
      → Discovers your Creation (the complete Method)
        → Enters through Crossing entry point
```

### Lead Capture Strategy

| Approach | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| Fully gated (email required) | High-value output worth exchanging email for |
| Partially gated (preview free, full results gated) | Balance of reach and capture |
| Ungated + optional email | Maximum reach, brand play |

### Bridge Copy (after tool use)

Use Climax language:
> "You just completed [Phase 1 assessment]. Your results show [Before State insight]. Want the complete [Method Name] to achieve [After State]? [Crossing CTA]."

## MVP Scope

### Minimum Viable Tool
1. Core functionality only — does the one thing
2. Essential UX — clear input, obvious output, mobile works
3. Basic lead capture — email, leads go somewhere
4. Bridge to Creation — clear next step

### What to Skip Initially
Account creation, saving results, advanced features, perfect design
