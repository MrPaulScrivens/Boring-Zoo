# World Code Email Templates

Reference templates organized by sequence type. Every template uses World Code elements as placeholders. Apply [[Scrivs Voice]] rules to all drafts.

**Voice reminders for all emails:**
- Lead with personal stake, not "You've been told..."
- Paragraphs no longer than 2 sentences
- No em dashes, colons, or filler phrases
- Concede before dismantling conventional wisdom
- Read it out loud. If it sounds like a marketing email, rewrite it.

---

## Welcome Sequence (5 Emails)

### Email 1: "Welcome to the World"

**Purpose:** Introduce core message, share transformation promise (Climax), establish Voice.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "So you want [Climax outcome]"
- "I'm going to be straight with you about [Climax topic]"
- "[First name], welcome to [World name]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> I started [World name] because [personal origin story in 1-2 sentences]. That one decision changed everything about how I [Climax topic area].

**Body structure:**
1. Personal stake. Why you built this world. What was broken for you. (2-3 short paragraphs)
2. State [Core Message] plainly. No buildup, no "here's the thing." Just say it.
3. Paint [Climax: After State] in specific, tangible terms. Not "more confidence." What does the person's Tuesday look like?
4. Acknowledge where [Character] is right now. Show empathy without pity.

**CTA:**
- Soft. No sell. Point them to one piece of content that represents your world.
- "If you want to see what I mean, start here: [link to cornerstone content]"

**Bridge to Email 2:**
> Tomorrow I'm going to challenge something you probably believe about [Climax topic area]. It's the one thing that kept me stuck for years.

**Voice notes:** This email sets the tone for everything. If it reads like a corporate welcome email, scrap it. The reader should feel like they just got a message from someone who gives a damn.

---

### Email 2: "The Wrong Belief"

**Purpose:** Challenge [Conversation: Wrong Belief], show why conventional approaches fail.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The [Climax topic] advice that's actually hurting you"
- "Why [Wrong Belief stated as common wisdom] is backwards"
- "I believed this for [X] years. I was wrong."

**Opening paragraph:**
> I used to think [Wrong Belief stated as personal past belief]. Spent [time period] doing it that way. Want to know what I got? [Honest, specific result of following Wrong Belief].

**Body structure:**
1. Share your own experience believing [Conversation: Wrong Belief]. Be specific about what you did and what happened.
2. Concede what's partially true about [Wrong Belief]. Give credit where it's due.
3. Then show the gap. What [Wrong Belief] misses. What it can't account for. Use a concrete example or analogy.
4. Plant the seed of [Method: Core Principle] without naming it yet. Let them feel the shift before you label it.

**CTA:**
- Reply-based. Invite conversation.
- "Hit reply and tell me: have you ever felt like [specific frustration caused by Wrong Belief]?"

**Bridge to Email 3:**
> There's a different way to approach [Climax topic]. I've been using it for [time period] and it works even when [common obstacle Character faces]. I'll walk you through it tomorrow.

**Voice notes:** Concede before dismantling. The reader believed [Wrong Belief] for a reason. Respect that. Then dismantle it with your experience, not with condescension.

---

### Email 3: "The Method Reveal"

**Purpose:** Introduce [Method] by name, share [Core Principle], tease phases.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The [Method Name] (and why it works)"
- "How [Method Name] fixes [Wrong Belief outcome]"
- "I call it [Method Name]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> After [X years/attempts/failures] of trying to [Climax outcome], I figured out something. [Core Principle stated in one sentence]. I built a whole method around it.

**Body structure:**
1. Name [Method] directly. Don't bury it. "[Method Name] is how I [Climax outcome]."
2. Explain [Method: Core Principle] in plain language. One paragraph max.
3. List [Method: Phases] briefly. Not a full breakdown. Just enough to show there's a real structure.
   - Phase 1: [Name] - one sentence on what it does
   - Phase 2: [Name] - one sentence on what it does
   - Phase 3: [Name] - one sentence on what it does
4. Share one quick result. Yours or someone else's. Specific numbers or outcomes.

**CTA:**
- Content-based. Link to a piece that demonstrates one phase in action.
- "I wrote about [Phase X] in detail here: [link]. It's the phase most people skip and it's the one that matters most."

**Bridge to Email 4:**
> Knowing the method is one thing. Seeing it work on a real person is another. Tomorrow I'll share a story that'll make this click.

**Voice notes:** This is where you get to geek out a little. You built this method. Be proud of it without being arrogant. Analogies work well here. "It's like..."

---

### Email 4: "The Climax Story"

**Purpose:** Share transformation story showing [Climax: Before State] to [Climax: After State].

**Subject line formulas:**
- "From [Before State detail] to [After State detail]"
- "[Name]'s story (this one stuck with me)"
- "What [specific timeframe] of [Method Name] looks like"

**Opening paragraph:**
> [Name/You] was [Before State: specific, vivid detail]. Not the dramatic version. The real one. The one where [mundane but painful daily reality of Before State].

**Body structure:**
1. Paint [Climax: Before State] with specificity. Not "struggling." What did struggling look like at 6am on a Wednesday?
2. The turning point. What changed? Keep it honest. No "and then everything clicked overnight."
3. What [Method] looked like in practice. Which [Phase] mattered most? What was hard?
4. [Climax: After State] with equal specificity. Same level of detail as Before. What does Tuesday look like now?
5. The thing nobody talks about. What was surprising about the transformation? What didn't change?

**CTA:**
- Soft sell. Mention [Creation] exists without hard pitching.
- "This is exactly what [Creation: Offer Name] is designed to do. If you're curious, I'll tell you more about it tomorrow."

**Bridge to Email 5:**
> I built something specifically for people in [Before State]. If that's you right now, tomorrow's email is the one that matters.

**Voice notes:** This email lives or dies on specificity. Vague transformation stories are noise. "She made more money" means nothing. "She went from $800/month freelancing to $4,200/month with two retainer clients" means everything.

### Email 5: "The Invitation"

**Purpose:** Introduce [Creation] offer using [Crossing] invitation language.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "[Creation: Offer Name] is open"
- "An invitation (if you're ready)"
- "This is for [Character] who want [Climax outcome]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> I'm not going to pretend this email isn't about my offer. It is. But I'm also not going to pressure you into anything. [Creation: Offer Name] exists for a specific person at a specific moment. This email will help you figure out if that's you right now.

**Body structure:**
1. Restate [Core Message] in one sentence.
2. Introduce [Creation: Offer Name]. What it is in plain terms. Format, what's included. No hype language.
3. Apply BONES:
   - **Big:** "[Creation] solves [Big Problem] by [how]"
   - **Obvious:** "When you're done, you'll have [specific tangible outcome]"
   - **New:** "This isn't [conventional approach]. It's [Method Name] applied to [specific area]"
   - **Easy:** "The format is [format]. You can [ease statement]"
   - **Safe:** "[Safety mechanism: guarantee, support structure, risk reversal]"
4. Address [Crossing: Real Objection] head-on. Don't pretend it doesn't exist. Name it and respond honestly.
5. [Crossing: Readiness Signals]. Help them self-select.
   - "This is for you if [signal 1]"
   - "This is for you if [signal 2]"
   - "This is NOT for you if [disqualifier]"

**CTA:**
- Direct. Clear. One link.
- "[Creation: Offer Name] - [price if applicable] - [link]"

**Voice notes:** Crossing language is invitation, not persuasion. "When you're ready" not "Act now before it's too late." The reader should feel respected, not hunted. The album analogy applies here. They already know if they want this. You're just telling them where to find it.

## Method Reveal Emails (3 Templates)

### Template: Phase Spotlight

**Purpose:** Deep dive into one [Method: Phase].

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The [Phase Name] phase (most people skip this)"
- "Why [Phase Name] changes everything"
- "[Method Name], Phase [X]: [Phase Name]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> If I could only teach one part of [Method Name], it would be [Phase Name]. Not because the other phases don't matter. They do. But this is the one where [specific shift that happens].

**Body structure:**
1. What [Phase Name] is and why it exists in the method
2. What most people do instead (connects back to [Wrong Belief])
3. How to actually do [Phase Name]. Give real, actionable detail. Not "think about your goals." Specific steps.
4. One example or result from applying this phase

**CTA:** Link to related content or invite a reply with their experience.

---

### Template: "Why [Conventional Wisdom] Fails"

**Purpose:** Use [Method]'s differentiation to challenge common advice.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "Stop [conventional action]. Do [Method approach] instead."
- "Why [respected conventional approach] stopped working"
- "[Conventional wisdom] worked. Until it didn't."

**Opening paragraph:**
> [Conventional wisdom] makes sense on the surface. I followed it for [time period]. [Concession: what's true about it]. But it misses something fundamental about [Climax topic area].

**Body structure:**
1. State the conventional wisdom fairly. Concede what works about it.
2. Show where it breaks down. Use personal experience or client example.
3. Introduce [Method: Core Principle] as the missing piece. Not as a replacement. As the thing that makes everything else work.
4. Contrast: "With [conventional approach], you get [limited result]. With [Core Principle], you get [Climax-aligned result]."

**CTA:** Invite them to try one small shift based on [Core Principle] this week.

---

### Template: Core Principle

**Purpose:** Teach the one idea that ties [Method] together.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The one idea behind everything I teach"
- "[Core Principle] (in plain English)"
- "Every phase of [Method Name] comes back to this"

**Opening paragraph:**
> If [Method Name] had a heartbeat, it would be this: [Core Principle in one sentence]. Every phase, every tool, every result traces back to this single idea.

**Body structure:**
1. State [Core Principle] clearly
2. Show how it connects to [Phase 1], [Phase 2], [Phase 3]
3. Share the moment you discovered it. What were you doing? What changed?
4. Give them one way to apply it today. Practical, small, immediate.

**CTA:** Reply with what [Core Principle] means to them. Or link to a deeper resource.

## Climax Tension Emails (3 Templates)

### Template: Before/After Contrast

**Purpose:** Make [Climax: Before State] and [After State] viscerally real.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "[Time period] ago vs. today"
- "Two versions of [Character archetype]"
- "Same person. Different [specific metric or reality]."

**Opening paragraph:**
> [Time period] ago, my [specific daily reality in Before State]. This morning, [specific daily reality in After State]. Same person. Different [operating system / approach / belief].

**Body structure:**
1. Before State: A specific day. What happened. How it felt. What you did about it (probably nothing productive).
2. The gap. Don't explain the whole method here. Just acknowledge that something shifted.
3. After State: The same kind of day. What happens now. How it feels different. Be honest about what's still hard.
4. The point: [Climax] is not about perfection. It's about [Core Principle applied to daily life].

**CTA:** "If your [Before State equivalent] sounds familiar, [soft pointer to Creation or content]."

---

### Template: "The Moment Everything Changed"

**Purpose:** Transformation story that creates emotional resonance.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The moment I stopped [Before State behavior]"
- "It happened on a [mundane day/setting]"
- "I almost [missed/quit/gave up]. Then this happened."

**Opening paragraph:**
> It wasn't dramatic. No lightning bolt. No motivational speech playing in the background. I was [mundane setting] when [specific small moment that triggered the shift].

**Body structure:**
1. Set the scene. Ordinary moment. No hero's journey theatrics.
2. What you realized. State it simply.
3. What you did differently starting that day. One specific action.
4. What cascaded from that one change. Connect it to [Climax: After State].
5. Why this matters for [Character]. They don't need a dramatic moment. They need [what you actually needed].

**CTA:** "The shift doesn't require a breakdown. It requires [Core Principle]. Here's where to start: [link]"

---

### Template: Future Pacing

**Purpose:** Paint [After State] so vividly the reader can feel it.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "Imagine [specific After State moment]"
- "6 months from now"
- "What [After State] actually feels like (not what you think)"

**Opening paragraph:**
> Six months from now, you [specific After State action]. Not because you finally "figured it out." Because you [Core Principle applied as daily habit].

**Body structure:**
1. Walk them through a future day. Morning, afternoon, evening. Specific details tied to [Climax: After State].
2. Name what's different AND what's the same. They're still them. Just operating differently.
3. Name what's surprisingly hard about [After State]. Honesty builds trust.
4. Bring it back to today. "The distance between where you are and this? It's [Method Name]. It's [Core Principle]."

**CTA:** Pointer to [Creation] or to a free resource that starts the journey.

## Creation Offer Emails (3 Templates)

### Template: BONES-Structured Launch

**Purpose:** Full offer presentation using BONES framework.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "[Creation: Offer Name] is live"
- "I built this for [Character who is in Before State]"
- "[Offer Name]: [Climax outcome] through [Method Name]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> I spent [time period] building [Creation: Offer Name]. Not because the world needed another [category]. Because [Character] needed [specific unmet need]. And nothing out there was doing it right.

**Body structure:**
1. **The Problem (Big):** "[Character] keeps [Before State pattern] because [Wrong Belief or systemic gap]. [Offer Name] fixes that."
2. **The Outcome (Obvious):** "When you finish [Offer Name], you will have [tangible deliverable 1], [tangible deliverable 2], and [tangible deliverable 3]." No vague promises.
3. **The Approach (New):** "This isn't [conventional approach]. [Offer Name] uses [Method Name] to [differentiated process]. Specifically: [Phase 1 applied], [Phase 2 applied], [Phase 3 applied]."
4. **The Format (Easy):** "[Format: course/coaching/community/etc.]. [Time commitment]. [How it's delivered]. You can [ease statement]."
5. **The Safety (Safe):** "[Guarantee or risk reversal]. [Support structure]. [What happens if it doesn't work for them]."

**CTA:**
- Direct, single link
- "[Offer Name] - [Price] - [Link]"
- Below CTA: "Questions? Reply to this email. I read every one."

---

### Template: FAQ / Objection Handling

**Purpose:** Address [Crossing: Real Objection] directly.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The question everyone asks about [Offer Name]"
- "Is [Offer Name] right for you? (Honest answer)"
- "Before you decide on [Offer Name], read this"

**Opening paragraph:**
> Since I opened [Offer Name], the most common question has been: "[Crossing: Real Objection phrased as a question]." Fair question. I'd ask the same thing.

**Body structure:**
1. State [Crossing: Real Objection] in the reader's own language. Don't sanitize it.
2. Answer it honestly. If there's a limitation, say so.
3. Address 2-3 secondary objections in Q&A format:
   - "Q: [Common question about format/time/price]"
   - "A: [Direct answer. No spin.]"
4. Restate [Readiness Signals]:
   - "You're ready for this if [signal]"
   - "Hold off if [honest disqualifier]"

**CTA:** Same direct link. "If the answers check out: [Link]"

---

### Template: "Last Chance" (Non-Manipulative)

**Purpose:** Final invitation before close. Respects the reader.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "[Offer Name] closes [timeframe]"
- "Last call for [Offer Name] (then I'm moving on)"
- "Quick note about [Offer Name]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> [Offer Name] closes [date/timeframe]. I'm not going to manufacture urgency or tell you the price is going up to scare you. But I am closing the doors because [honest reason: starting the cohort, limiting group size, focusing on delivery].

**Body structure:**
1. Restate what [Offer Name] is in 2-3 sentences. Clear, no fluff.
2. Restate [Climax: After State] in one vivid sentence.
3. Acknowledge that not buying is fine. "If the timing isn't right, it isn't right. I'll still be here. The emails keep coming. [World name] isn't going anywhere."
4. For those who are ready: remove friction. Answer the one thing that might be stopping them.

**CTA:**
- "[Offer Name] - [Link] - Doors close [date]"
- No countdown timers. No fake scarcity.

**Voice notes:** This email should feel like a friend letting you know about a deadline, not a marketer squeezing a conversion. If you wouldn't say it across a table, don't write it.

## Ongoing Conversation Emails (3 Templates)

### Template: Content Theme Email

**Purpose:** Explore one [Conversation: Content Theme] to deepen the reader's connection to your world.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The [Content Theme] nobody talks about"
- "My take on [Content Theme]"
- "[Content Theme] and [surprising connection]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> I've been thinking about [Content Theme] a lot lately. Specifically, [personal angle or recent experience that brought it up].

**Body structure:**
1. Your take on [Content Theme]. Lead with opinion. Where do you stand?
2. Share an experience, observation, or story that illustrates your perspective.
3. Connect [Content Theme] back to [Core Message] or [Core Principle]. Show how this theme fits into your world.
4. Give the reader something to chew on. A question, a reframe, or a small action.

**CTA:** Reply-based or link to related content. "What's your experience with [Content Theme]? Hit reply."

---

### Template: Wrong Belief Dismantling

**Purpose:** Continue challenging [Conversation: Wrong Belief] with new angles.

**Subject line formulas:**
- "The [Wrong Belief] trap (part [X])"
- "Another reason [Wrong Belief] doesn't work"
- "I watched someone [follow Wrong Belief]. This happened."

**Opening paragraph:**
> I saw [specific scenario: a post, a conversation, a client situation] that reminded me why [Wrong Belief phrased as common advice] keeps people stuck.

**Body structure:**
1. Describe the scenario. Make it vivid but brief.
2. Identify the [Wrong Belief] operating underneath the scenario.
3. Show what [Core Principle] or [Method] would have changed about the outcome.
4. Don't be preachy. Frame it as "I've been there too" not "they should have known better."

**CTA:** Link to content that teaches the alternative. Or ask for their experience.

---

### Template: Reader Story / Proof

**Purpose:** Share real results to build trust and reinforce [Method].

**Subject line formulas:**
- "[Name] did [specific result] in [timeframe]"
- "Proof that [Core Principle] works"
- "A quick story about [Character type]"

**Opening paragraph:**
> [Name] sent me a message [recently]. [One sentence quote or paraphrase of their result]. I asked if I could share it because [reason it matters beyond the numbers].

**Body structure:**
1. Who they are. Enough context that [Character] sees themselves. "[Name] is a [relevant description] who was dealing with [Before State]."
2. What they did. Which part of [Method] did they apply? Be specific.
3. What happened. Results in concrete terms.
4. What surprised them (or you). The unexpected part builds credibility.
5. The lesson for the reader. Connect it back to [Core Principle] or [Conversation theme].

**CTA:** "If you want the same kind of result, [Creation: Offer Name] is where [Name] started: [link]." Or keep it soft with a content link.
