# Turning Walls & Themes into Content

A system for expanding each Wall (content pillar) and its struggles into 25+ content pieces that stay connected to your World Code.

## Content Layers Checklist

Before creating any content, know which layers you're hitting:

- **Layer 1: Outcomes, Problems, Mechanisms** — The practical layer. What's the result? What's the problem? How does it work?
- **Layer 2: Experiences, Opinions, Worldviews** — The personal layer. Your stories, your takes, your way of seeing things.
- **Layer 3: Identity, Transformation, Becoming** — The deep layer. Who is your person becoming?

**For long-form content:** Aim to weave all 3 layers. If you're missing one, the content will underperform.
**For short-form content:** 1-2 layers is fine. Pick the layer that fits the format.

### How the 5 Angles Map to Layers

| Angle | Primary Layer(s) | Why |
|-------|-----------------|-----|
| Teaching | Layer 1 | Outcomes, problems, mechanisms — the practical how-to |
| Story | Layer 2 | Your experiences, your perspective, your journey |
| Contrast | Layer 2 | Your opinions and worldview vs. conventional wisdom |
| Proof | Layer 1 + Layer 2 | Mechanisms that worked + the personal experience behind them |
| Future | Layer 3 | Identity, transformation, who they're becoming |

**To hit all 3 layers in long-form:** Combine angles. A Teaching post (L1) that includes your Story (L2) and paints the Future identity (L3) = powerful content.

## The Theme Expansion Framework

Every Bridge has 3 Walls, and each Wall has 3 Struggles. Walls are your content pillars — the territories your content covers. But a wall isn't a post. A wall is a well you draw from, and this framework shows you how to draw 25+ pieces from each one.

### Step 1: Wall/Theme to 5 Angles

For any Wall or Struggle, generate 5 angles. Each angle is a different lens on the same topic.

**1. The Teaching Angle** (Layer 1)
Explain the wall/struggle through your Method lens. What does your Code say about this? What principle or phase applies? This is you as the guide, showing the Character how things work inside your world.

**2. The Story Angle** (Layer 2)
Your personal experience with this wall/struggle. What happened to you? What did you see? What did you screw up? The Story Angle is where your Character sees themselves in you. It builds trust because you went first.

**3. The Contrast Angle** (Layer 2)
This wall/struggle vs. the Goblin voice. Conventional wisdom says one thing. Your world says another. The Contrast Angle draws a line in the sand. It polarizes, which is the point. People who agree move closer. People who disagree were never your Character.

**4. The Proof Angle** (Layer 1 + Layer 2)
Evidence. Results. Transformation stories. Your own or your clients'. The Proof Angle answers the question the Character is always silently asking: "Does this actually work?" Connect it to the Treasure for this wall. Show the before/after.

**5. The Future Angle** (Layer 3)
What changes when the Character scales this wall? Paint the picture of life on the other side. This isn't hype. It's showing them the Treasure — what becomes possible. Connect it to the identity shift your world promises.

### Step 2: Each Angle to Content Types

For each of the 5 angles, create content in these formats:

- **Hook post** — A single powerful statement or question. One sentence that stops the scroll. This is your sharpest version of the angle.
- **Thread/carousel** — Multi-part deep dive. Break the angle into 5-10 connected points. Each point should stand alone but gain power from the sequence.
- **Story post** — Narrative format. Beginning, middle, insight. The reader should feel like they lived something alongside you.
- **Teaching post** — Framework or how-to. Steps, principles, a system. The reader walks away knowing how to do something they didn't before.
- **Hot take** — Controversial or surprising perspective. Say the thing others won't. Challenge what's accepted. This is where your world's beliefs clash with the mainstream.

**5 angles x 5 content types = 25 pieces per wall.**

With 3 walls, that's 75 pieces. And each struggle within a wall can be expanded the same way for even more depth.

You'll never run out of content again. You'll run out of time before you run out of material.

Connection to Method: The Depth Over Reach principle (Phase 2 of Method)

- Hook post: "1,000 followers making $20k/month beats 100,000 followers making $2k/month."
- Thread: "Why small audiences are more profitable than big ones (a breakdown):"

  Step 1: Stop optimizing for reach. Start optimizing for replies.

  Step 3: Make one offer. Make it easy to say yes.

## Content Calendar Integration

### Rotating Walls Across the Weekly Loop

If your rhythm is daily posting, assign each wall a primary day:

```
3 Walls:
- Monday/Thursday: Wall 1
- Tuesday/Friday: Wall 2
- Wednesday/Saturday: Wall 3
- Sunday: Flex (whichever wall needs reinforcement or a Crossing-focused post)
```

### Rotating Angles Within Each Wall

Don't burn through all 5 angles on one wall before touching the others. Rotate angles weekly:

```
Week 1: Teaching Angle across all walls
Week 2: Story Angle across all walls
Week 3: Contrast Angle across all walls
Week 4: Proof Angle across all walls
Week 5: Future Angle across all walls
```

This gives you 5 weeks of content before you repeat an angle. When you do repeat, use a fresh example, a different story, or a deeper layer of the same principle.

### Balancing Wall Depth vs. Variety

- **Go deep on one wall per month.** Pick the wall most connected to your current Crossing offer. Give it extra posts, more story angles, more proof. This creates momentum around a single idea.
- **Touch every wall at least once per week.** Even if one wall is getting the deep treatment, the others need to stay visible. A single hook post or hot take keeps them alive.
- **Let audience response guide depth.** If a wall consistently gets more engagement, give it more airtime. Your audience is telling you what matters to them.

### When to Repeat Angles with Fresh Examples

Repeat an angle when:
- You have a new story or result that illustrates it better
- You've noticed new followers who haven't seen the original
- A current event or cultural moment makes the angle freshly relevant
- You've refined your thinking since the last version

Never repeat the same post. Always bring a new example, a sharper insight, or a different format.

### Weaving in Crossing Entry Points

Your Crossing is the entry point to your world. It should appear naturally, not forced.

- **Teaching posts:** End with "This is what I walk people through inside [Creation name]."
- **Story posts:** Close with "If this resonates, [Crossing invitation]."
- **Proof posts:** The result itself is the Crossing pitch. "Here's how [person] got started: [entry point]."
- **Future posts:** Paint the picture, then offer the path. "This is what becomes possible. [Crossing language]."

Aim for 1 in 5 posts to include a direct Crossing reference. The other 4 build the trust and belief that make the Crossing feel inevitable.

## Communication Style Adaptation

Your Conversation includes a communication style. Here's how each style adapts the templates above.

### Stories Style

Every angle leads with narrative. Even teaching posts start with a scene.

- Hook posts become story openers: "The day I realized [wall insight] started at 6am in a parking lot."
- Threads become serialized stories with lessons embedded in the narrative
- Teaching posts frame every step as something that happened to you or someone you know
- Hot takes come from lived experience, not theory: "I tried [conventional approach]. It cost me [specific consequence]."

The key: the lesson is always discovered, never declared.

### Frameworks/Teaching Style

Every angle leads with structure. Even story posts include a model or system.

- Hook posts become principle statements: "[Method name] Principle #3: [Sharp statement]."
- Threads become step-by-step breakdowns with clear headers and numbered points
- Story posts are case studies with a visible framework applied
- Hot takes challenge other frameworks directly: "[Popular system] misses the most important variable."

The key: everything is learnable, repeatable, and structured.

### Raw Honesty Style

Every angle leads with vulnerability. Nothing is polished.

- Hook posts are confessions: "I still struggle with [wall topic]. But I know something now that I didn't before."
- Threads are unfiltered accounts of what actually happened, including the parts that don't look good
- Teaching posts acknowledge what you still don't know alongside what you do
- Hot takes are uncomfortable truths about yourself, your industry, or your audience

The key: the reader trusts you because you don't pretend.

### Humor + Insight Style

Every angle leads with a sharp observation that makes people laugh before it makes them think.

- Hook posts are observations with a twist: "I spent $5,000 on a course that taught me what my grandma said for free."
- Threads alternate between jokes and genuine insights, keeping the reader off-balance
- Teaching posts use absurd analogies to make complex ideas stick
- Hot takes are delivered with enough humor that people share them before they realize they've been challenged

The key: the humor is the delivery system for the worldview. It lowers defenses so the idea can land.

## Quick Reference: The Math

```
1 Wall
x 5 Angles (Teaching, Story, Contrast, Proof, Future)
x 5 Content Types (Hook, Thread, Story Post, Teaching Post, Hot Take)
= 25 content pieces per wall

3 Walls = 75 pieces
+ Each struggle within a wall can be expanded the same way
= Effectively unlimited content from your Bridge
```

That's a minimum of 75 content pieces from your Bridge alone. And each one is connected to your Climax, filtered through your Method, and building toward your Crossing.

No random content. No "what should I post today?" Every piece comes from your Bridge and leads back to it.
