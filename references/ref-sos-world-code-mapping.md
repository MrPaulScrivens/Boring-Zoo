# Soap Opera Sequence + World Code Mapping

This file maps Andre Chaperon's Soap Opera Sequence (SOS) beats to World Code elements, then provides detailed email-by-email templates for all three sequence types.

---

## The Five SOS Beats

| Beat | Purpose | World Code Fuel | Reader Feels |
|------|---------|----------------|--------------|
| **Hook** | Grab attention, create immediate emotional connection | Climax (Before State) + Conversation (Wrong Belief) | "This person knows exactly what I'm going through" |
| **Backstory** | Build trust, reveal your why, earn the right to teach | Voice (authenticity) + Origin story + Crown positioning | "I understand why they care so much" |
| **Wall** | Show the obstacle at full force — the lowest point | Conversation (Wrong Belief at peak) + Climax (Before State at worst) | "Oh no. This is the part I'm afraid of" |
| **Epiphany** | The discovery moment — what changed everything | Method (core principle, discovery) | "Wait — THAT's what I've been missing?" |
| **Resolution** | Show the after, present the path, invite them forward | Climax (After State) + Creation + Crossing | "I want that. And there's a clear path" |

---

## How World Code Fuels Each Beat

### Hook
The hook doesn't start with you. It starts with them. Pull from:
- **Climax Before State**: The specific daily reality of their struggle
- **Conversation Wrong Belief**: The thing they believe that's keeping them stuck
- **Climax Transformation Promise**: Hint at where this is going

The hook works because it mirrors. The reader sees themselves in your opening lines. No teaching yet. No method. Just "I see you."

### Backstory
Now it's about you — but only because your story serves them. Pull from:
- **Voice**: Your authentic way of speaking, your personality
- **Origin story**: How you got here, why you care
- **Crown**: Your positioning — what makes you the person to listen to

The backstory earns trust. It answers the unspoken question: "Why should I listen to you?" Not with credentials. With shared experience.

### Wall
The wall is the Before State at its worst — the moment where the wrong belief was fully in control and everything felt hopeless. Pull from:
- **Conversation Wrong Belief**: Show what happens when you follow it all the way
- **Climax Before State**: The most vivid, specific, painful version
- **Voice Authenticity Markers**: Be raw here. This is where vulnerability builds connection.

The wall creates empathy and tension simultaneously. The reader fears this moment AND recognizes it.

### Epiphany
The turning point. Something shifted. Pull from:
- **Method Core Principle**: The one idea that unlocked everything
- **Method Discovery Story**: How you found it (not how you teach it)
- **Method Differentiation**: Why this approach is different from what they've tried

The epiphany is NOT a lecture. It's a moment. A realization. Something clicked. Show the click, not the curriculum.

### Resolution
The after. The proof. The path forward. Pull from:
- **Climax After State**: What life looks like now, specifically
- **Creation**: The offer that packages the transformation
- **Crossing**: The invitation language, readiness signals, buying experience

The resolution satisfies the story AND opens a door. It doesn't hard-sell. It presents a clear path for people who are ready.

## Sequence Type 1: Origin Story (5 emails, 7-10 days)

The default sequence. Use for welcome/nurture when someone joins your list. This is the story of how you discovered your worldview and built your method.

### Email 1: The Hook
**SOS Beat**: Hook
**World Code**: Climax (Before State) + Conversation (Wrong Belief)
**Send**: Immediately on signup
**Goal**: Mirror the reader's current reality, create "this person gets me" feeling

**Structure**:
1. Open with a vivid Before State scene — your version of their current reality (2-3 sentences)
2. Name the frustration without solving it. Show you've lived it.
3. Challenge the conventional wisdom briefly: "Everyone says [Wrong Belief]. I used to believe that too."
4. Hint at the transformation: "Something changed. And it wasn't what I expected."
5. Deliver whatever they signed up for (lead magnet, resource, etc.)

**Subject line direction**:
- Pull from Climax tension: "So you want [After State outcome]"
- Mirror Before State: "If you're tired of [Before State pattern]..."
- Challenge: "The [topic] advice that's keeping you stuck"

**Open loop** (Culprit Tease):
Close by hinting at the real enemy — the deeper reason they're stuck — without naming it. Tease tomorrow's email where you'll go deeper into your story.

**Tone**: Direct, empathetic, no hype. The reader should feel seen, not sold to.

---

### Email 2: The Backstory
**SOS Beat**: Backstory
**World Code**: Voice + Origin story + Crown positioning
**Send**: Day 2-3
**Goal**: Build trust through shared experience, earn the right to teach

**Opens by resolving**: The Culprit Tease from Email 1. Name the deeper problem in your first 2-3 lines.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve the previous loop: Name the real enemy / deeper reason
2. Tell your origin story — how you got into [Climax topic area]
3. What you believed at the start (Wrong Belief, from your own experience)
4. What you tried that didn't work (builds credibility through failure)
5. The moment things started to get really hard (setup for the Wall)

**Subject line direction**:
- Story pull: "How I ended up [unexpected place]"
- Personal: "I need to tell you something about [topic]"
- Honest: "I was wrong about [topic] for [time period]"

**Open loop** (Story Cliff):
Stop your story at the moment of peak tension — the lowest point, the biggest failure, the wall you hit. Don't resolve it. Cut to black.

**Tone**: Vulnerable, honest. Voice authenticity markers at full strength. This email sounds like a real person talking, not a marketer.

---

### Email 3: The Wall
**SOS Beat**: Wall
**World Code**: Conversation (Wrong Belief at full force) + Climax (Before State at worst)
**Send**: Day 4-5
**Goal**: Show the lowest point, create empathy and tension, make them feel the weight of the problem

**Opens by resolving**: The Story Cliff from Email 2. Pick up the story exactly where you left off.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve the cliffhanger: Continue the story from the moment of tension
2. Show the wall in full — the moment the Wrong Belief was fully in control
3. What it felt like. What you almost did (quit, gave up, went back to the old way)
4. The specific detail that made it real (not dramatic, mundane — the 6am Wednesday version)
5. Then: the crack. The small thing that started the shift. Don't explain the full epiphany yet.

**Subject line direction**:
- Tension: "The moment I almost quit [topic]"
- Raw: "This is the part I don't usually talk about"
- Continuation: "So there I was..."

**Open loop** (Method Preview):
Name your Method or its core principle for the first time. Mention that you discovered something — a different way — but don't explain how it works. Just name it and tease that it changed everything.

**Tone**: Raw, specific, grounded. No hero's journey theatrics. The wall should feel real and relatable, not dramatic and performative.

---

### Email 4: The Epiphany
**SOS Beat**: Epiphany
**World Code**: Method (core principle, discovery, differentiation)
**Send**: Day 6-7
**Goal**: Deliver the "aha" moment, introduce the Method as the bridge from Before to After

**Opens by resolving**: The Method Preview from Email 3. Explain the core principle or phase you teased.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Explain the Method principle you named yesterday
2. The discovery moment — when and how you figured this out
3. Why this is different from what they've tried (Method differentiation)
4. Give them one actionable insight from the Method — a quick win they can apply today
5. Show how this connects to the bigger transformation (Climax After State preview)

**Subject line direction**:
- Discovery: "The [Method Name] (and why it works)"
- Reframe: "What if [Wrong Belief opposite] was the answer?"
- Direct: "I call it [Method Name]. Here's why."

**Open loop** (Promise of Proof):
Tease a specific transformation story — someone who applied this and got results. Name details (timeframe, specific outcome) without telling the full story. Promise to share it tomorrow.

**Tone**: Energized but grounded. You're sharing something you genuinely believe in. Geek out a little. But keep it accessible — this is a conversation, not a lecture.

---

### Email 5: The Resolution
**SOS Beat**: Resolution
**World Code**: Climax (After State) + Creation + Crossing
**Send**: Day 8-10
**Goal**: Close the story, show the transformation, present the path forward

**Opens by resolving**: The Promise of Proof from Email 4. Share the transformation story you teased. ALL remaining loops close in this email.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Tell the transformation story (yours or someone else's — Before State → Method → After State)
2. Paint the After State specifically. What does the transformed Tuesday look like?
3. Connect back to where the reader is now: "If you're still in [Before State], here's what I want you to know..."
4. Introduce your Creation (offer) as the path. Use BONES if applicable. Keep it honest.
5. Crossing language: Invitation, not persuasion. Readiness signals. "This is for you if..."
6. Close the loop on the full story — where you are now, having gone through all of this

**Subject line direction**:
- Resolution: "The other side of [Before State]"
- Invitation: "If you're ready for [After State outcome]"
- Story close: "How the story ends (and what's next for you)"

**No open loop.** This email closes everything. The reader should feel complete — whether they buy or not.

**Tone**: Warm, clear, respectful. Crossing language is invitation, not pressure. The reader should feel like they have a genuine choice. "When you're ready" not "Act now."

## Sequence Type 2: Method Reveal (4 emails, 5-7 days)

Use when teaching your framework through story. The audience already knows you somewhat — this sequence goes deeper into how your Method works by showing it in action.

### Email 1: The Problem Nobody Names
**SOS Beat**: Hook + Backstory (compressed)
**World Code**: Conversation (Wrong Belief) + Voice
**Send**: Day 1
**Goal**: Reframe the problem, name what everyone else misses

**Structure**:
1. Start with the conventional approach to [topic]. What everyone does.
2. Name why it doesn't work — not that it's bad, but that it's incomplete
3. Share your own experience following the conventional path
4. Name your Method as the thing that fills the gap — but don't teach it yet

**Open loop** (Method Preview):
Name the first phase or principle. Explain WHAT it is but not HOW it works. Promise to break it down tomorrow.

---

### Email 2: The Framework in Action
**SOS Beat**: Wall + Epiphany (compressed)
**World Code**: Method (phases, core principle, differentiation)
**Send**: Day 2-3
**Goal**: Teach the Method through a real story, not as a lecture

**Opens by resolving**: The Method Preview. Teach the phase/principle you teased.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Walk through the first phase/principle with a real example
2. Show what happens when someone applies it (story format, not bullet points)
3. Introduce the next phase — how it builds on what they just learned
4. Give an actionable takeaway they can try today

**Open loop** (Contradiction):
State something that seems to contradict what you just taught. "But here's where it gets counterintuitive..." Promise to explain why tomorrow.

---

### Email 3: The Counterintuitive Part
**SOS Beat**: Epiphany (deepened)
**World Code**: Method (differentiation) + Conversation (Wrong Belief dismantling)
**Send**: Day 4-5
**Goal**: Deliver the insight that separates your Method from everything else

**Opens by resolving**: The Contradiction. Explain why the seemingly contradictory thing is actually essential.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Explain the contradiction. Why both things are true.
2. This is the core differentiator of your Method — the thing that makes it work when other approaches fail
3. Share the "oh" moment — when you (or a student) realized this was the missing piece
4. Connect it to their Before State: "This is why [Before State pattern] keeps happening"

**Open loop** (Promise of Proof):
Tease specific results. Numbers, timeframes, transformation details. Promise the full story tomorrow.

---

### Email 4: The Full Picture
**SOS Beat**: Resolution
**World Code**: Climax (After State) + Creation + Crossing
**Send**: Day 6-7
**Goal**: Close with proof, present the full Method, invite them forward

**Opens by resolving**: The Promise of Proof. Share the full transformation story.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Tell the full results story
2. Recap the Method: What it is, how the phases connect, why it's different
3. The After State — what becomes possible when the Method is applied consistently
4. Present your Creation as the best way to learn/apply the Method
5. Crossing invitation with readiness signals

**No open loop.** Complete resolution.

---

## Sequence Type 3: Creation Launch (5 emails, 5-7 days)

Use when launching or promoting a specific offer. The story arc is about WHY you built this thing and WHO it's for.

### Email 1: Why I Built This
**SOS Beat**: Hook
**World Code**: Voice + Climax (Before State of your audience)
**Send**: Day 1
**Goal**: Establish the origin of the offer, create emotional investment

**Structure**:
1. Open with the moment you decided to build this Creation
2. What you saw in your audience that told you this was needed
3. The specific Before State pain that existing solutions weren't solving
4. Your personal stake: why this matters to you beyond business

**Open loop** (Story Cliff):
Stop at the moment of decision — you knew what you wanted to build but you weren't sure it would work. Cut to black.

---

### Email 2: The Problem Goes Deeper
**SOS Beat**: Backstory + Wall
**World Code**: Conversation (Wrong Belief) + Climax (Before State at worst)
**Send**: Day 2
**Goal**: Show the depth of the problem this Creation solves

**Opens by resolving**: The Story Cliff. What happened when you started building.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Continue the building story
2. The deeper problem you discovered while creating this — it wasn't just [surface problem], it was [root cause]
3. Why existing solutions miss this (Wrong Belief applied to the market/industry)
4. How this realization changed what you built

**Open loop** (Culprit Tease):
Name the root cause but don't explain how your Creation addresses it. "I redesigned the entire [component] around this insight. I'll show you what that looks like tomorrow."

---

### Email 3: How It Works Differently
**SOS Beat**: Epiphany
**World Code**: Method + Creation (how it's built, what makes it different)
**Send**: Day 3-4
**Goal**: Show the Creation's approach, connect it to your Method

**Opens by resolving**: The Culprit Tease. Show how the Creation addresses the root cause.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Walk through the key design decision / approach
2. How the Creation applies your Method — which phases, which principles
3. What's included (BONES: Big problem, Obvious outcome, New approach, Easy format, Safe guarantee)
4. One specific component or module that you're most proud of — and why

**Open loop** (Promise of Proof):
Tease early results — beta users, early adopters, your own experience using it. Specific numbers or outcomes without the full story.

---

### Email 4: The Proof
**SOS Beat**: Resolution (part 1)
**World Code**: Climax (Before → After transformation)
**Send**: Day 4-5
**Goal**: Show real results, build belief that this works

**Opens by resolving**: The Promise of Proof. Share the full results story.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Full transformation story from someone who used the Creation
2. Before State → what they did → After State (specific, vivid, honest)
3. What surprised them about the experience
4. Address the main objection (Crossing Real Objection) through the story, not through argument
5. If no external stories yet, use your own experience applying what you built

**Open loop** (Method Preview — light):
"Tomorrow I'll share exactly who this is for (and who it's not for). And I'll answer the question I keep getting." Keep it simple — the heavy lifting is done.

---

### Email 5: The Invitation
**SOS Beat**: Resolution (complete)
**World Code**: Creation + Crossing
**Send**: Day 5-7
**Goal**: Present the full offer, invite action, close all loops

**Opens by resolving**: The light preview from Email 4. Answer the common question. State who this is for.

**Structure**:
1. Resolve: Readiness signals — "This is for you if... This is NOT for you if..."
2. Full offer presentation: what's included, format, price, timeline
3. Address remaining objections honestly
4. Crossing language: Invitation, not urgency. Respect the reader's timing.
5. Close the full story arc — connect back to Email 1's origin moment. "This is why I built it. If it's right for you, the door is open."

**No open loop.** Complete resolution.

## Adapting Beats to Tone Level

Not every sequence needs maximum drama. Adjust the intensity of each beat based on the audience's sophistication and your Voice.

**High intensity** (new audience, emotional topic):
- Wall = raw vulnerability, specific pain
- Epiphany = dramatic realization moment
- Hook = vivid Before State painting

**Medium intensity** (warm audience, practical topic):
- Wall = honest challenge, specific obstacle
- Epiphany = clear insight, "here's what I figured out"
- Hook = relatable frustration

**Low intensity** (sophisticated audience, technical topic):
- Wall = intellectual challenge, "the gap in the model"
- Epiphany = framework introduction, "here's the missing piece"
- Hook = contrarian observation

The SOS structure stays the same at all intensity levels. Only the emotional register changes.
