# World Code Social Media Post Templates

Templates built around World Code framework elements. Every post should connect back to your World, not just chase engagement.

**World Code elements used in templates:**
- **Voice** — Your tone, rhythm, authenticity markers
- **Climax** — Your transformation promise (before state to after state)
- **Method** — Your named methodology, core principle, phases
- **Creation** — Your offer built on the BONES framework
- **Conversation** — Your core message, wrong belief, content themes
- **Crossing** — Your entry point, invitation language

Fill in `[bracketed placeholders]` with your specific World Code elements.

## Method Posts

### 1. Phase Breakdown Post

> I spent [time period] figuring out [Problem your Method solves].
>
> It came down to [number] phases.
>
> **Phase 1: [Phase Name]**
> [One sentence on what happens in this phase and why it matters]
>
> **Phase 2: [Phase Name]**
> [One sentence on what happens in this phase and why it matters]
>
> **Phase 3: [Phase Name]**
> [One sentence on what happens in this phase and why it matters]
>
> [Optional: Phase 4+]
>
> [Method Name] works because [one sentence on the underlying principle].

### 2. Core Principle Post

> [Method Name] is built on one idea.
>
> [State the core principle in one sentence.]
>
> [Explain why this principle matters using a personal example or analogy. 2-3 short paragraphs.]
>
> Once you understand [Core Principle], [Result that changes how they operate].

### 3. Contrast Post

> Most people try to [Common Approach] when they want [Desired Outcome].
>
> I get it. [One sentence acknowledging why the common approach makes sense on the surface.]
>
> But [Common Approach] breaks down when [specific scenario or truth they haven't considered].
>
> [Method Name] does something different.
>
> Instead of [Common Approach], you [Method's Alternative Approach].
>
> [One sentence on the result this produces.]

### 4. Method Origin Story

> [Method Name] didn't start as a method.
>
> It started when [specific moment, failure, or realization that sparked the idea].
>
> [2-3 short paragraphs telling the story of how the method evolved. Include at least one mistake or wrong turn.]
>
> That's when I realized [Core Insight].
>
> [One sentence connecting the story to what the method does for people now.]

### 5. Quick Tip From One Phase

> Quick [Method Name] tip from [Phase Name].
>
> [State the tip in one direct sentence.]
>
> Why this works: [1-2 sentences explaining the reasoning.]
>
> Try this today: [One specific, actionable step they can take right now.]

>

>

>

>
> [2-3 sentences on what you learned and how your behavior changed.]
>

>
> What works instead: [Replacement Approach — tied to your Method or Core Principle].

>

## Voice-Calibrated Hooks

Use these as opening lines. The hook is the first thing people see. It determines whether they read the rest.

### Direct/Blunt Hooks

1. `[Wrong Belief] is keeping you broke.`
2. `Stop [Common Behavior]. It's not working.`
3. `I wasted [time period] on [thing you abandoned]. Don't repeat my mistake.`
4. `[After State] doesn't require [thing everyone assumes it requires].`
5. `The fastest way to [Desired Outcome] is to stop [counterintuitive thing to stop].`

### Warm/Encouraging Hooks

1. `If you're struggling with [Before State symptom], this is going to help.`
2. `The [After State] you want is closer than you think. But the path isn't what you expect.`
3. `[Time period] ago I was exactly where you are with [Problem]. Let me show you what changed.`
4. `You don't need another [common solution]. You need [what your Method actually provides].`
5. `I wish someone had told me this when I was stuck in [Before State].`

### Intellectual/Analytical Hooks

1. `There's a pattern behind every [Desired Outcome] I've seen. It has [number] parts.`
2. `[Content Theme] works differently than people think. The mechanism is [Core Principle].`
3. `I've tested [number] approaches to [Problem]. Only [number] actually moved the needle.`
4. `The difference between [Before State] and [After State] isn't effort. It's [Core Principle].`
5. `[Method Name] came from a question: what if [Core Principle reframed as a question]?`

### Playful/Irreverent Hooks

1. `I accidentally [achieved result] by doing the opposite of what every [guru/expert] told me.`
2. `My [Method Name] process looks lazy. It works anyway.`
3. `Everyone's overcomplicating [Topic]. It's [number] steps. That's it.`
4. `I'm probably wrong about a lot of things. But not about [Core Principle].`
5. `The internet told me to [Common Advice]. I did [what you actually did] instead.`

## Platform-Specific Formatting Notes

### X/Twitter

**Single tweet:** 280 characters max. Use hooks from above. One idea only.

**Thread structure:**
- Tweet 1: Hook (strongest line from your post)
- Tweet 2: Context or story setup
- Tweets 3-6: Core content (one idea per tweet)
- Tweet 7: Summary or takeaway
- Final tweet: Soft CTA or restate the principle

**Tips:**
- Break paragraphs into individual tweets at natural pause points
- Each tweet should stand alone if someone sees it out of context
- First tweet determines whether anyone reads the rest
- No hashtags (they look desperate on X)

### Instagram

**Caption structure:**
- Line 1: Hook (shows before "...more")
- Line 2-3: Story or context
- Body: Core content with line breaks between every 1-2 sentences
- Close: Question, CTA, or principle restatement

**Carousel framework (for Method and Transformation posts):**
- Slide 1: Hook or bold claim (text on branded background)
- Slides 2-7: One point per slide, large readable text
- Final slide: Summary + where to learn more

**Tips:**
- First line of caption is everything (it's the hook before the fold)
- Use line breaks aggressively for readability
- Carousels outperform single images for educational content
- Save-worthy content > like-worthy content

### LinkedIn

**Post structure:**
- Line 1: Hook (shows before "see more")
- Line 2: Empty line (forces the fold break after hook)
- Body: Short paragraphs, line breaks between each
- Close: Reflective question or clear takeaway

**Tips:**
- 1,200-1,500 characters performs well
- No external links in post body (kills reach, put in comments)
- Document/carousel posts get strong organic reach
- Personal stories with business lessons outperform pure tactics
- Method Posts and Wrong Belief Posts perform especially well here

### Newsletter

**Adapting social posts to long-form:**
- Use social post as the skeleton, then expand each section
- Add personal stories and context that don't fit in short-form
- Method Posts expand naturally into tutorials
- Transformation Posts expand into case studies
- Wrong Belief Posts expand into essay-length arguments
- Include one clear CTA per email (tied to your Creation/Offer)

**Structure:**
- Subject line: Use a hook from above
- Opening: First 2-3 sentences from the social post
- Body: Expanded version with more story, more detail, more nuance
- Close: Tie back to your World, CTA to your Crossing point
