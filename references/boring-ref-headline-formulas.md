# World Code Headline Formula Reference

A comprehensive library of fill-in-the-blank headline templates organized by World Code element. Every formula uses `[placeholders]` that map directly to fields in your `world-code/` files.

Use this reference when writing headlines for content, sales pages, emails, social posts, or ads. Pull from the section that matches your intent.

---

## Climax-Based Headlines (Before/After Transformation)

These headlines leverage the tension between the Before State and After State from `world-code/climax.md`. They work because transformation is the most compelling promise you can make.

### Formulas

1. **From [Before State] to [After State] in [Timeframe]**
2. **What [Before State] People Get Wrong About [Transformation Promise]**
3. **You Don't Have a [Surface Problem] Problem. You Have a [Before State] Problem.**
4. **The Real Reason You're Still [Before State]**
5. **[After State] Is Closer Than You Think — Here's What's Actually in the Way**
6. **I Was [Before State] Until I Realized [Core Insight About Transformation]**
7. **Stop Trying to Fix [Before State]. Start Building [After State].**
8. **What [After State] Actually Looks Like (It's Not What You Think)**
9. **The Gap Between [Before State] and [After State] Is Smaller Than You Believe**
10. **Why [Who This Is For] Stay Stuck in [Before State] (And What to Do Instead)**
11. **[Before State] Is Not Your Fault. Staying There Is.**
12. **Nobody Talks About the Ugly Middle Between [Before State] and [After State]**
13. **The [Transformation Promise] Nobody Prepared You For**
14. **What I'd Tell [Who This Is For] Who's Stuck in [Before State] Right Now**
15. **[After State] Doesn't Require What You Think It Requires**
16. **How [Who This Is For] Go From [Before State] to [After State] Without [Common Sacrifice]**
17. **The Moment Everything Changed: From [Before State] to [After State]**

### Examples

**Fitness coach** (Before State: "constantly starting over on diets" / After State: "eating without rules and staying lean"):
- "From Constantly Starting Over on Diets to Eating Without Rules and Staying Lean in 90 Days"
- "You Don't Have a Willpower Problem. You Have a Constantly-Starting-Over Problem."
- "The Real Reason You're Still Constantly Starting Over on Diets"

- "Stop Trying to Fix Your Idea Problem. Start Building One Offer That Pays."

## Method-Based Headlines (Naming & Phases)

## Creation-Based Headlines (BONES Offer)

These headlines sell your offer from `world-code/creation.md` using the BONES framework: **B**ig (solves a real problem), **O**bvious (clear outcome), **N**ew (different from what they've tried), **E**asy (simple to execute), **S**afe (low risk). Each formula emphasizes one or more BONES elements.

### Formulas

1. **[Offer Name]: [Obvious Outcome] Without [Common Risk]** (Obvious + Safe)
2. **The Simplest Path to [After State] — No [Hard Thing] Required** (Easy + Obvious)
3. **Why [Offer Name] Works When [Conventional Approach] Didn't** (New)
4. **[Offer Name] Is Not Another [Category They've Tried]. Here's the Difference.** (New)
5. **Get [Obvious Outcome] Even If You've [Common Past Failure]** (Safe)
6. **[Big Problem]? [Offer Name] Was Built for This.** (Big)
7. **[Number] [Who This Is For] Have Used [Offer Name] to [Obvious Outcome]** (Big + Obvious)
8. **What Makes [Offer Name] Different From Every [Category] You've Tried** (New)
9. **[Offer Name]: [Obvious Outcome] in [Timeframe] — Here's How** (Obvious + Easy)
10. **No [Complex Prerequisite]. No [Time Commitment Fear]. Just [Obvious Outcome].** (Easy + Safe)
11. **[Offer Name] Removes the [Biggest Barrier] Between You and [After State]** (Easy + Big)
12. **Try [Offer Name]. If [Guarantee/Safety Condition], [Safety Net].** (Safe)
13. **You've Never Approached [Problem] This Way Before. That's the Point.** (New)

### Examples

**Online course** (Offer: "Revenue Engine" / Obvious Outcome: "your first $5k month" / Big Problem: "smart creators making no money"):
- "Revenue Engine: Your First $5k Month Without Building a Massive Audience" (Obvious + Safe)
- "The Simplest Path to Your First $5k Month — No Viral Content Required" (Easy + Obvious)
- "Revenue Engine Is Not Another Marketing Course. Here's the Difference." (New)

**Coaching program** (Offer: "The Rewire" / Obvious Outcome: "stop self-sabotaging before every breakthrough" / Big Problem: "high performers who keep hitting invisible ceilings"):
- "The Rewire: Stop Self-Sabotaging Before Every Breakthrough Without Years of Therapy" (Obvious + Safe)
- "Get Control of Self-Sabotage Even If You've Done All the Mindset Work" (Safe)
- "Hitting Invisible Ceilings? The Rewire Was Built for This." (Big)

## Voice Application Rules

Every headline you write passes through your Voice filter from `world-code/voice.md`. These rules are non-negotiable.

### How to Apply Voice to Any Headline

1. **Write the headline using the formula.** Get the structure and message right first.
2. **Read it out loud.** Does it sound like something you would actually say? If it sounds like a marketing template, rewrite it in your words.
3. **Apply your Hard Rules.** Check every word against your non-negotiable Voice rules. If a rule says "no exclamation points," remove them. If a rule says "no jargon," strip it.
4. **Match your Tone & Character.** If your tone is "direct and blunt," cut the hedging. If your tone is "warm and conversational," soften the edges. The formula gives you structure; the Voice gives you personality.
5. **Check Authenticity Markers.** Does this headline include the quirks, patterns, or language choices that make your writing unmistakably yours?
6. **Verify Sentence Rhythm.** Does the headline match your natural cadence — short and punchy, or flowing and layered?

### Tone Calibration Checklist

Before publishing any headline, run through this:

- [ ] Does this sound like me or like a copywriting template?
- [ ] Would I say this to someone face-to-face without cringing?
- [ ] Does this follow every Hard Rule in my Voice file?
- [ ] Is the tone consistent with my Tone & Character description?
- [ ] Have I used my vocabulary, not generic marketing language?
- [ ] Does this match the rhythm and sentence structure patterns from my Voice?
- [ ] If someone who knows my work read this, would they recognize it as mine?
- [ ] Am I being honest, or am I exaggerating for clicks?

### Common Voice Violations to Watch For

- **Generic power words** ("unlock," "supercharge," "skyrocket") — replace with your natural language
- **Exclamation points** — almost always unnecessary; remove unless your Voice explicitly uses them
- **Passive constructions** — rewrite in active voice
- **Hedging qualifiers** ("just," "maybe," "kind of") — either commit to the statement or cut it
- **Someone else's catchphrases** — use your own terminology from your Method and Conversation

## How to Use This Reference

1. **Pick the section that matches your intent.** Writing content? Start with Conversation-Based. Writing a sales page? Start with Crossing-Based and Creation-Based. Writing a hook for social media? Start with Climax-Based.
2. **Fill in the placeholders** using your actual `world-code/` file content.
3. **Run the result through Voice Application Rules** before publishing.
4. **Mix formulas across sections.** A great sales page headline might combine a Climax-Based structure with a Creation-Based BONES element. A great content headline might blend Conversation-Based wrong belief framing with a Method-Based phase reference.
5. **Test multiple versions.** Write 3-5 headline variations for anything important. The first one is rarely the best one.
