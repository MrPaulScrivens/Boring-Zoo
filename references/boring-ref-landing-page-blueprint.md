# World Code Landing Page Blueprint

A section-by-section landing page blueprint where every element maps directly to the World Code framework. Before using this blueprint, ensure you have completed all six World Code elements: Voice, Climax, Method, Creation, Conversation, and Crossing.

---

## Section 1: Hero — maps to Climax

The hero is your Climax compressed into a single screen. The visitor should immediately know: what transformation you promise, who it is for, and what to do next.

### Structure

- **Headline:** The Transformation Promise from your Climax. Concrete, visualizable, specific enough to measure.
- **Subheadline:** Before State to After State bridge. One sentence that names where they are now and where they will be.
- **CTA button:** Invitation Language from your Crossing. Not "Buy Now" — the words you would actually use to invite someone.
- **Social proof indicator:** One credibility marker. A number, a recognizable name, a specific result. Keep it tight.

### Template

```
[Headline: Transformation Promise — concrete, visualizable outcome]

[Subheadline: You're stuck in [Before State]. [Offer Name] takes you to [After State]
in [timeframe].]

[CTA button: Invitation Language from Crossing]

[Social proof: "X people have [specific result]" or "[Name] went from [before] to [after]"]
```

### Voice Check

- Does the headline sound like something YOU would say? Read it out loud.
- Does it follow your Hard Rules? No banned words or phrases.
- Does it match your Tone & Character? If your voice is blunt, the headline should be blunt. If your voice is warm, the headline should be warm.

## Section 2: Problem — maps to Climax Before State + Conversation Wrong Belief

This section proves you understand their world better than they do. You are not listing features or benefits yet. You are describing their current reality so precisely that they think you are reading their mind.

### Structure

- **Before State vivid description:** Paint the daily experience of being stuck. Use sensory, specific language. What does a bad Tuesday look like for this person?
- **Wrong Belief callout:** Name the lie from your Conversation element. The thing everyone else tells them that does not work. This is the enemy of your world.
- **Empathy bridge:** Show you understand because you have been there, or because you have seen it a hundred times. Not pity — recognition.

### Template

```
## [Headline that names the frustration — from Before State]

[2-3 sentences painting the Before State. Specific. Sensory. Daily reality, not abstract
problems.]

[1-2 sentences naming what they have tried. The conventional wisdom. The advice that
sounds right but keeps them stuck.]

The problem isn't [surface-level issue].

The problem is [Wrong Belief from Conversation] — the idea that [expand on the lie:
what it tells them to do, why it feels true, why it fails].

[1-2 sentences of empathy bridge. You get it because [your connection to this
experience]. Not "I understand your pain" — show it.]
```

### Voice Check

- The Before State description should use their language, not marketing language. Pull from how your person would describe it to a friend (Climax Question 5).
- The Wrong Belief should feel like a revelation, not an accusation.

---

## Section 3: Bridge — maps to Method Introduction

The bridge is where you introduce a different way of thinking. You are not selling yet. You are shifting their frame. The Method name and Core Principle do the heavy lifting here.

### Structure

- **Method introduction by name:** State the name of your methodology. Naming it gives it weight.
- **Core Principle:** The one idea from your Method that changes everything. This is the thread that runs through all your phases.
- **Why It Works Differently:** Direct contrast with the conventional approach (which you just dismantled in the Problem section). Not "better" — different.

### Template

```
## There's a different way. It's called [Methodology Name].

[Core Principle — stated as a single, clear idea. One sentence. Maybe two. This is the
lens that changes how they see the problem.]

Most [people in your space] tell you to [conventional approach from Method Q2].

[Methodology Name] works because [Why It Works Differently — what makes your
approach succeed where conventional methods fail].

[1-2 sentences connecting the Core Principle back to the Transformation Promise. If they
adopt this principle, the Climax becomes inevitable.]
```

### Voice Check

- The Method name should feel natural coming out of your mouth, not like a branded trademark.
- The Core Principle should be simple enough for someone to repeat it to a friend.

---

## Section 4: Method — maps to Method Phases

Walk through your phases as a journey, not a curriculum. Each phase answers three questions: what do they do, what changes, and what do they gain. The reader should feel momentum building — each phase makes the next one possible.

### Structure

- **Phase walkthrough:** Present each phase with a clear name, what happens, what shifts, and the outcome of that phase.
- **Journey arc:** Build anticipation. Phase 1 unlocks Phase 2. Phase 2 makes Phase 3 possible. The sequence matters.
- **Micro-transformations:** Each phase delivers a small win that proves the Method works before they reach the full Climax.

### Template

```
## How [Methodology Name] Works

### [Phase 1 Name]
[What they do in this phase — specific actions, not vague concepts]
[What changes — the shift that happens]
[What they gain — the micro-win that proves it's working]

### [Phase 2 Name]
[What they do]
[What changes]
[What they gain]

### [Phase 3 Name]
[What they do]
[What changes]
[What they gain]

[Continue for each phase — typically 3-5 total]

By the end: [Restate the Transformation Promise — what the full journey delivers]
```

### Voice Check

- Phase descriptions should sound like you explaining your process to a client, not a course outline.
- Each phase name should be memorable and distinctly yours.

## Section 5: Offer — maps to Creation

This is where you present what they are buying. Structure the entire section around the BONES framework from your Creation element. Every piece of information should map to Big, Obvious, New, Easy, or Safe.

### Structure

- **Offer Name and Format:** Lead with the name and how it is delivered.
- **BONES presentation:**
  - **B (Big):** The big problem this solves — pull directly from your Climax Before State. Make the stakes clear.
  - **O (Obvious):** The obvious outcome — your Climax Transformation Promise restated as what they walk away with.
  - **N (New):** What is new and different — your Method's differentiation. Why this is not another version of what they have already tried.
  - **E (Easy):** How it is easy to execute — your program structure, the format, the support level. Remove the "can I actually do this?" fear.
  - **S (Safe):** Why it is safe to say yes — risk reversal, guarantees, proof, community, access length. Remove the "what if it doesn't work?" fear.
- **Program structure:** What is included, mapped to your Method phases.
- **Pricing:** The number and the rationale behind it. What the price signals about the transformation and the commitment.

### Template

```
## Introducing [Offer Name]

[One sentence: Format + who it is for + what it delivers]

### What you get:

[Program structure — map each item to a Method phase. For each item, state what it is
and what it does for them.]

- **[Phase 1 module/component]** — [what it covers and why it matters]
- **[Phase 2 module/component]** — [what it covers and why it matters]
- **[Phase 3 module/component]** — [what it covers and why it matters]
- **[Support element]** — [what kind of access/guidance and how it helps]
- **[Bonus/additional element if applicable]** — [what it is and why it is included]

### Why this works when other things haven't:

[N from BONES — 2-3 sentences on what makes this different. Pull from Method's
"Why It Works Differently."]

### Your investment:

[Price point]

[Pricing rationale — 1-2 sentences on what this price signals and why it is right for this
level of transformation. Reference the Creation pricing rationale.]

[CTA button: Invitation Language from Crossing]

### Your safety net:

[S from BONES — guarantee, refund policy, risk reversal. State it plainly. No fine print
energy.]
```

### Voice Check

- The offer description should sound like you telling a friend what you built, not a features list.
- Pricing rationale should be honest, not defensive. If the price is high, own it. If it is accessible, say why.

## Section 6: Proof — maps to Climax After State Evidence

Proof is not testimonials pasted on a page. It is evidence that the Climax is real. Every piece of proof should demonstrate the Before State to After State transformation.

### Structure

- **Transformation stories:** Before/After format. Name the Before State, name what changed, name the After State. Specific results, not vague praise.
- **Results that demonstrate the Climax:** Numbers, timelines, specific outcomes that map to your Transformation Promise.
- **Variety:** Different people, different starting points, same transformation. This proves the Method works across situations.

### Template

```
## People who made the crossing

### [Name or description]
**Before:** [Their specific Before State — what they were stuck in]
**After:** [Their specific After State — what changed]
**In their words:** "[Direct quote that captures the transformation]"

### [Name or description]
**Before:** [Before State]
**After:** [After State]
**In their words:** "[Quote]"

### [Name or description]
**Before:** [Before State]
**After:** [After State]
**In their words:** "[Quote]"

[Optional: Summary stat — "X people have [achieved specific result] using
[Methodology Name]"]
```

### Voice Check

- Proof should feel like stories, not sales collateral.
- If you do not have testimonials yet, use your own transformation story or a detailed case study of how the Method works in practice.

## Section 7: Objections — maps to Crossing Real Objection

Address the real objection from your Crossing element directly. Do not hide it in fine print. The right person needs to hear you acknowledge their fear and respond honestly.

### Structure

- **Lead with the real objection:** The one thing that stops the RIGHT person from saying yes (from Crossing Q5).
- **Honest response:** Not a rebuttal — an acknowledgment and a genuine answer. Connect it to the BONES "Safe" element.
- **FAQ:** Common concerns that are secondary to the real objection. Each answer should connect back to the World Code elements.

### Template

```
## The thing you're probably thinking

"[Real Objection from Crossing — stated in their words, not yours]"

[Honest response — 2-4 sentences. Acknowledge the fear. Explain why it is valid. Then
explain specifically how [Offer Name] addresses it. Reference the "Safe" element from
BONES.]

### Other questions:

**"[Common question 1 — about format/time commitment]"**
[Answer — connect to Creation format and support level]

**"[Common question 2 — about whether it works for their situation]"**
[Answer — connect to Method's "Why It Works Differently" and proof/results]

**"[Common question 3 — about price/value]"**
[Answer — connect to Climax transformation and pricing rationale]

**"[Common question 4 — about guarantee/risk]"**
[Answer — restate BONES "Safe" element plainly]

**"[Common question 5 — about timing/readiness]"**
[Answer — connect to Crossing readiness signals]
```

### Voice Check

- The real objection should be stated in the exact words your person would use, not sanitized marketing language.
- Answers should be direct. No hedging, no corporate non-answers.

---

## Section 8: Final CTA — maps to Crossing Invitation

The final call to action is an invitation, not a close. If everything above has done its job, the right person already wants to say yes. Your job is to make the path clear.

### Structure

- **Invitation language:** From your Crossing element. The words you would actually say to invite someone in.
- **Buying experience:** Describe what happens after they click. Remove uncertainty about the process (from Crossing Q4).
- **Final Climax restatement:** One last articulation of the transformation. Not a new pitch — a reminder of what is waiting on the other side.

### Template

```
## [Invitation — not "Act Now" or "Don't Miss Out." Something you would actually say.]

[Final Climax restatement — 1-2 sentences. The After State, vivid and concrete. What
their life looks like on the other side of this transformation.]

[CTA button: Invitation Language from Crossing]

[Buying experience — what happens next: "Click the button. [Describe the process —
application, checkout, welcome email, onboarding]. You'll [first thing they experience]
within [timeframe]."]

[Optional closing line — personal, human, in your Voice. Something that sounds like
YOU, not a landing page.]
```

### Voice Check

- The invitation should feel like an open door, not a countdown timer.
- The buying experience description should eliminate every "what happens if I click?" anxiety.

## Voice Application Checklist

Run through this checklist for every section of the page before publishing.

### Tone & Character
- [ ] Every section matches your natural tone from Voice
- [ ] The energy is consistent from top to bottom — no sudden shifts into corporate speak or hype
- [ ] It sounds like you talking, not a template filled in

### Hard Rules
- [ ] No banned words or phrases appear anywhere on the page
- [ ] No banned punctuation patterns
- [ ] No banned structural patterns (e.g., if you hate listicles, the page should not read like a listicle)

### Authenticity Markers
- [ ] The intangible qualities from your Voice are present — the rhythm, the energy, the feel
- [ ] You could read this page out loud to a friend without cringing
- [ ] Personal examples, stories, or asides are included where natural

### Consistency
- [ ] The Methodology Name is used consistently (same capitalization, same phrasing)
- [ ] The Offer Name is used consistently
- [ ] The Transformation Promise is stated the same way each time (minor variations are fine, contradictions are not)

### Banned Patterns (Universal)
- [ ] No false urgency ("Only 3 spots left!" unless literally true)
- [ ] No manufactured scarcity
- [ ] No guilt-based CTAs
- [ ] No pressure tactics disguised as empathy
- [ ] No generic testimonials without specifics

## Page Variations

### Long-Form Sales Page (Full Blueprint)

Use all eight sections in order. This is for high-ticket offers ($500+), complex transformations, or audiences who need significant trust-building before purchase.

**Sections:** Hero → Problem → Bridge → Method → Offer → Proof → Objections → Final CTA

**When to use:**
- Premium or high-ticket Creation
- New audience who does not know you yet
- Complex Method that needs explanation
- Transformation that requires proof

---

### Short-Form Landing Page

Compressed version for simpler offers, warm audiences, or direct response.

**Sections:** Hero → Problem → Offer → CTA

**Template:**

```
[Hero: Headline + Subheadline + CTA — from Climax]

[Problem: 3-5 sentences painting the Before State + Wrong Belief — from Climax +
Conversation]

[Offer: What it is + what is included + price + safety net — from Creation BONES]

[CTA: Invitation Language + buying experience — from Crossing]
```

**When to use:**
- Lower-ticket offers ($47-$197)
- Warm audience who already knows your Method
- Simple, clear transformation
- Retargeting or email traffic (they already know you)

---

### Webinar / Workshop Registration Page

Entry point page designed to get people into a live or recorded experience of your Method.

**Sections:** Hero → Problem (compressed) → What They Will Learn → CTA

**Template:**

```
[Hero: "[Workshop/Webinar Name]: How to [Micro-Transformation related to
Climax]"]

[Subheadline: "A free [format] where I'll walk you through [Core Principle from
Method] — the [one idea] that [specific shift it creates]."]

[Problem — 2-3 sentences: "If you're [Before State] and you've tried [Wrong Belief
approach] without results..."]

## What you'll walk away with:
- [Takeaway 1 — maps to Method Phase 1 or Core Principle]
- [Takeaway 2 — maps to a quick win from the Method]
- [Takeaway 3 — maps to a mindset shift related to Wrong Belief]

[CTA: "Save your spot" or equivalent Invitation Language]

[Date/time/format details]
```

**When to use:**
- Building an audience before a launch
- Warming cold traffic
- Demonstrating Method credibility
- Lead generation for high-ticket Creation

---

### Entry Point / Lead Magnet Page

The simplest page. Gets someone from "curious" to "in your world." Maps directly to the Crossing entry point.

**Sections:** Headline → What They Get → CTA

**Template:**

```
[Headline: "[Entry Point Name]: [Specific quick win related to Climax]"]

[1-2 sentences: What this is, what it delivers, and how fast they will see a result.
Connect to Method — this is a taste of your approach.]

[Optional: 3 bullet points of what is inside]

[CTA: "Get [Entry Point Name]" — simple, direct]

[What happens next: "Enter your email. You'll get [Entry Point] immediately. Then
[what they can expect — first email, first lesson, first result]."]
```

**When to use:**
- Top of funnel — first touch
- Growing your email list
- Giving people a taste of your Method before presenting the Creation
- Entry point defined in your Crossing element

## How to Use This Blueprint

1. **Read your six World Code files first.** Voice, Climax, Method, Creation, Conversation, Crossing. Every placeholder in this blueprint maps to a specific element in those files.
2. **Fill in the templates section by section.** Do not skip sections for the long-form page. Each section does a specific job.
3. **Run the Voice Application Checklist** after drafting. Read the entire page out loud. Fix anything that does not sound like you.
4. **Start with the variation that matches your situation.** If you do not have testimonials yet, use the short-form. If you are launching a high-ticket offer to a cold audience, use the full long-form.
5. **Revisit and refine.** The first version will not be perfect. Use real feedback and conversion data to tighten the copy. The blueprint stays the same — the words get sharper.
