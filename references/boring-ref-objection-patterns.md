# World Code Objection Handling Reference

This reference maps common objections to specific World Code elements so every response stays rooted in the framework rather than generic sales tactics.

Key principle from [[9. The World Code - One Crossing]]: You are inviting, not convincing. If you have to twist someone's arm, they aren't your One Character.

## Crossing Objections (The Real Objection)

The Crossing captures ONE real objection from the right person. Not a list of twenty worries. The one fear that sits between them and the entry point.

Before responding to any objection, ask: **Is this the real objection or a surface-level deflection?** If it's not the real one, no template will help. You need to find the actual fear first.

### Acknowledgment + BONES "Safe" Response

1. Name the fear directly. No sugarcoating.
2. Connect it to the "Safe" element of your Creation. What makes this low-risk for them?
3. Offer proof that the risk is handled (guarantee, structure, access level, etc.)

**Formula**: "I get it. [Fear stated back plainly]. That's why [Creation Name] is built so that [specific Safe element]. [Proof or structure that removes risk]."

### Story-Based Response

1. Tell a transformation story where the person had the exact same fear.
2. Show what happened when they moved forward anyway.
3. Let the outcome speak. Don't moralize about it.

**Formula**: "[Person/I] felt the same way. [One sentence on their Before State]. [What they did]. [Their After State, stated simply]."

### Evidence-Based Response

1. Lead with the specific data point or result.
2. Connect it directly to their fear.
3. Keep it short. Evidence doesn't need a sales pitch.

**Formula**: "[Specific result]. [How it addresses their specific fear]. [Optional: how many others experienced similar]."

### Invitation Reframe

1. Flip the objection into the reason the offer exists.
2. Show that the offer was designed FOR people with this exact concern.
3. Keep the door open. No pressure.

**Formula**: "[Offer Name] exists because of [their objection]. It was built for people who [feel/experienced their fear]. [Invitation, not ultimatum]."

## Common Objection Categories with World Code Responses

### "I've tried this before and failed"

This is the most valuable objection. It tells you the person has the desire (good) but has been burned (your opportunity to differentiate).

**Method Differentiation Response**
- Name the conventional approach they likely tried.
- Contrast it with your Code's core principle and why it works differently.
- The goal is to make them see a genuinely new path, not a repackaged version of the old one.

Template: "Most [conventional approaches] fail because [root cause]. [Method Name] works differently because [Why It Works Differently from One Code]. It's not about [old approach]. It's about [your Code's core principle]."

**Climax Reframe**
- Connect their past failure to the Before State in your Climax.
- Show that "tried and failed" IS the Before State. They aren't behind. They're exactly who this is for.

Template: "The fact that you've tried before and it didn't stick? That's the Before State. [Method Name] was built for people in that exact spot, not for people starting fresh with zero experience."

---

### "I can't afford it right now"

Respect the financial reality. Never guilt someone into spending money they don't have. But do make the cost of inaction visible.

**Creation Value Reframe (BONES "Big")**
- Reframe the price against the cost of staying stuck.
- Use the "Big" element. What is the big problem costing them right now?
- Don't be manipulative. State the math plainly.

Template: "I respect that. But think about what [big problem] is costing you right now. Not just money, but [time/energy/opportunity]. [Creation Name] is [price] to solve a problem that's already costing you [cost of inaction]."

**Entry Point Alternative (from Crossing)**
- Offer a taste of the world first. Lower the barrier.
- This could be free content, a smaller offer, a community entry point.
- The World Current concept: let them ride the current through your world until they're ready.

Template: "No pressure at all. In the meantime, [free resource / smaller entry point] will give you a real taste of how [Method Name] works. When the timing is right, [Creation Name] will be here."

---

### "I'm not sure it'll work for me"

This objection is about identity, not information. They don't believe they're the kind of person who gets results.

**Method Phases Response**
- Walk them through the phases of your Code.
- Show specifically how each phase applies to someone in their situation.
- Make the path feel concrete and doable (BONES "Easy").

Template: "Here's how it breaks down for someone in your position. Phase 1: [what they'd do first]. Phase 2: [next step]. Phase 3: [where it clicks]. You're not guessing. You're following a path that's been mapped."

**Transformation Proof (Before/After State)**
- Share a specific Before/After that mirrors their situation.
- The closer the match to their circumstances, the more powerful.
- Keep it factual. Let them see themselves in it.

Template: "[Person] was in a similar spot. [Their Before State, specific]. After going through [Method Name], [their After State, specific]. The difference wasn't talent or luck. It was having the right Code."

---

### "I need to think about it"

This is either the real objection hiding behind politeness, or genuine processing time. Both are fine.

**Non-Pushy Crossing Response**
- Honor their process. No countdown timers. No "spots are filling up."
- Restate what's available and when.
- Leave the door open and move on.

Template: "Take whatever time you need. [Creation Name] isn't going anywhere. If questions come up while you're thinking it over, [how to reach you]. I'd rather you come in ready than rushed."

**Future Pacing (Climax After State)**
- Paint the After State briefly. Not as pressure but as a picture.
- Let them sit with the vision of what changes.
- One or two sentences max. Don't write a novel.

Template: "[Time period] from now, you could be [After State, specific and tangible]. That's worth sitting with for a bit. No rush."

---

### "I can figure this out on my own"

They probably can. That's not the point. The point is speed, structure, and not wasting years on trial and error.

**Method Core Principle Response**
- Acknowledge they could figure it out. Don't argue.
- Identify what they're actually missing. Not information (the internet has that). The methodology. The sequence. The structure.

Template: "You probably can. The information is out there. What's not out there is [your Code's unique element]. That's the difference between knowing what to do and having a system that makes it happen. [Method Name] is the system."

**Wrong Belief Connection (from Conversation)**
- This objection often connects to the wrong belief your Conversation addresses.
- Name the wrong belief directly.
- Show how your world sees it differently.

Template: "The belief that more information solves the problem is [the Wrong Belief, stated plainly]. [Your Conversation's core message]. [Method Name] isn't more information. It's [what it actually is]."

## Voice Application in Objection Handling

### Maintaining Authentic Tone

- Write objection responses the same way you write content. Short paragraphs. Simple words. Personal stake first.
- Lead with your own experience or opinion, not with what the prospect "has been told."
- If you wouldn't say it in a coffee conversation, don't write it in a sales response.
- Concede what's true about their concern before showing why your approach is different. This isn't weakness. It's credibility.

### Anti-Manipulation Checklist

Before sending any objection response, verify:

- [ ] Am I inviting or convincing? (If convincing, rewrite.)
- [ ] Would I say this to a friend? (If not, rewrite.)
- [ ] Am I creating false urgency? (If yes, remove it.)
- [ ] Am I guilt-tripping about their current situation? (If yes, rewrite.)
- [ ] Am I respecting their "no" as a valid answer? (If not, stop.)
- [ ] Does this sound like me or like a sales script? (If script, rewrite.)
- [ ] Am I leading with my perspective or with "you've been told"? (If the latter, flip it.)

### Hard Rules for Sales Responses

- No em dashes, en dashes, or colons unless absolutely necessary.
- No conversational filler ("Here's the thing," "Let me be honest," "Look,").
- No meta-commentary about what you're about to say. Just say it.
- No "That's not X, that's Y" reframes. Make your point directly.
- No fake scarcity. If there's real scarcity, state it once and move on.
- No "just" minimizing ("It's just $97," "Just try it for a week"). State the price and the value plainly.
- Paragraphs no longer than 2 sentences in any response format.

## Objection Response Templates

### "I've tried this before and failed"

**Short-form (DM/comment reply)**

"Most approaches to [topic] are built the same way. [Method Name] isn't. It works because [one-line differentiator]. Happy to show you what's different if you're curious."

**Medium-form (email response)**

"I hear you. And honestly, the fact that you've tried before tells me you're serious about [Climax]. Most people never even start.

The problem with [conventional approach] is [root cause of failure]. It's not your fault it didn't work. It wasn't built for the way [your Character] actually operates.

[Method Name] takes a different angle. Instead of [old way], you [new way based on your Code]. That one shift changes everything.

If you want to see how it'd play out for your situation, [low-friction next step]."

**Long-form (sales page section)**

"You've tried [conventional approach]. Maybe more than once. And every time, it started strong and fizzled out. That's not a you problem. That's a method problem.

[Conventional approaches] are built on [wrong assumption]. They work great for a certain type of person in a certain situation. But for [your Character], they fall apart because [specific reason].

[Method Name] was built differently. The core principle is [Why It Works Differently]. Instead of [old sequence], you follow [your Code's phases, briefly].

[Transformation story. Before State. What they did. After State.]

[Specific result number or proof point.]

That's not a fluke. That's what happens when the method actually fits."

---

### "I can't afford it right now"

**Short-form (DM/comment reply)**

"Totally respect that. [Free resource or entry point] is a solid place to start in the meantime. No cost, real value. [Link]"

**Medium-form (email response)**

"I appreciate you being straight with me about that.

I'm never going to pressure you into something that doesn't feel right financially. That would go against everything [your world] stands for.

What I will say is this. [Big problem] doesn't pause while you save up. It keeps costing you [specific cost of inaction].

If now isn't the time for [Creation Name], grab [free resource / smaller offer]. It'll give you a real head start using [Method Name]. When the budget opens up, you'll already know if this is right for you."

**Long-form (sales page section)**

"Let's talk about price honestly.

[Creation Name] is [price]. That's real money. I'm not going to pretend it's nothing.

But here's what I want you to weigh it against. [Big problem] is already costing you. Every month you spend [doing the wrong thing / staying stuck], you're paying [specific cost: lost revenue, wasted time, missed opportunity]. Not to me. To the problem itself.

[Creation Name] at [price] solves a problem that's costing you [larger amount] over [time period]. The math favors action.

But if the timing genuinely isn't right, I get it. [Entry point / free resource] is here for you. No commitment. Real value. Start there and come back when you're ready.

[Invitation, not ultimatum]."

[BONES Safe element: guarantee, support structure, or risk reducer]."

[Restate what's waiting for them. After State. Specific and tangible.]

No countdown timers. No vanishing bonuses. When you're ready, the door is open."

### "I can figure this out on my own"

**Short-form (DM/comment reply)**

"You probably can. The question is how long it takes. [Method Name] is the shortcut through the trial and error. But either way, I'm rooting for you."

**Medium-form (email response)**

"I believe you can. Seriously. The information is everywhere.

What took me [time period] to figure out wasn't the information. It was the order. The structure. Knowing what to ignore and what to double down on.

[Method Name] is [time period] of trial and error compressed into [your Code's format]. That's what you're really paying for. Not information. Sequence.

But if you want to go solo, [free resource] is a solid starting point. Use it. See how far it takes you."

**Long-form (sales page section)**

"You can absolutely figure this out on your own. People do it every day.

It takes most people [realistic time period]. A lot of that time isn't spent learning. It's spent unlearning. Trying something, realizing it doesn't work, pivoting, trying again.

[Wrong Belief from your Conversation]. That belief is why the solo path takes so long. Not because you're slow. Because you're solving the wrong problem.

[Method Name] exists because I already walked that path. [Personal story of your own trial-and-error journey, brief]. I built [Method Name] so that [your Character] doesn't have to repeat that process.

[Your Code's phases, briefly. Show the sequence they'd skip to.]

The information is free. The methodology is [Creation Name]."
