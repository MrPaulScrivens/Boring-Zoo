## Core Principles

### 1. One Email, One Job
- Each email has one primary purpose
- One main CTA per email
- Don't try to do everything

### 2. Value Before Ask
- Lead with usefulness
- Build trust through Conversation theme content
- Earn the right to sell

### 3. Walk Them Into Your World
- Each email should deepen their understanding of your World Code
- Move them from Before State → awareness of your Method → desire for the Climax transformation
- The sequence IS the journey

### 4. Clear Path Forward
- Every email moves them toward the Crossing
- Links should do something useful
- Make next steps obvious using Crossing language

## World Code Welcome Sequence

The most important sequence. This walks new subscribers into your World.

**Length**: 5-7 emails over 12-14 days
**Goal**: Build belief in your worldview, activate, convert

### Email Flow

1. **Welcome + Deliver Value** (immediate)
   - Deliver whatever they signed up for
   - Introduce yourself in your Voice
   - Hint at the Climax transformation
   - *World Code: Voice + Climax promise*

2. **The Before State** (day 1-2)
   - Paint the Before State vividly (from `climax.md`)
   - Show you understand where they are
   - Challenge the Wrong Belief (from `conversation.md`)
   - *World Code: Climax Before State + Conversation Wrong Belief*

3. **The Origin Story** (day 3-4)
   - Your story: how you discovered the Method
   - Why you believe what you believe (Core Message from `conversation.md`)
   - Build connection through vulnerability (Voice Authenticity Markers)
   - *World Code: Voice + Conversation Core Message*

4. **The Method Introduction** (day 5-6)
   - Introduce your Method by name
   - Explain Why It Works Differently
   - Give a quick win from Phase 1
   - *World Code: Method name + phases*

5. **Social Proof / Transformation Story** (day 7-8)
   - Share a Before → After story
   - Show the Climax transformation in someone else
   - Build belief that change is possible
   - *World Code: Climax transformation*

6. **The Deeper Problem** (day 9-11)
   - Address the Real Objection from `crossing.md`
   - Go deeper on why the Before State persists
   - Position your Method as the bridge
   - *World Code: Crossing Real Objection + Method*

7. **The Invitation** (day 12-14)
   - Present your Creation (offer)
   - Use BONES breakdown to show value
   - CTA using Crossing Buying Experience language
   - Reference the transformation promise one more time
   - *World Code: Creation + Crossing + Climax*

## Lead Nurture Sequence

**Length**: 6-8 emails over 2-3 weeks
**Goal**: Build trust through Conversation themes, convert

### Email Flow

1. **Deliver lead magnet + intro** (immediate) — Voice, Climax promise
2. **Expand on Conversation Theme 1** (day 2-3) — Educational, in Voice
3. **Problem deep-dive** (day 4-5) — Before State pain, Wrong Belief challenge
4. **Method framework introduction** (day 6-8) — Method phases as solution
5. **Transformation story** (day 9-11) — Climax Before → After
6. **Method differentiation** (day 12-14) — Why It Works Differently
7. **Address Real Objection** (day 15-18) — Crossing objection handling
8. **Direct offer** (day 19-21) — Creation with Crossing CTA

## Re-Engagement Sequence

**Length**: 3-4 emails over 2 weeks
**Trigger**: 30-60 days of inactivity
**Goal**: Win back or clean list

1. **Check-in** — Genuine concern, in Voice (not generic "we miss you")
2. **Value reminder** — Share best Conversation theme content + Method insight
3. **Reconnect to Climax** — Remind of the transformation promise
4. **Last chance** — Honest close (stay or unsubscribe), in Voice

## Email Copy Guidelines

### Structure
1. **Hook**: First line grabs attention — pull from Climax tension
2. **Context**: Why this matters to someone in the Before State
3. **Value**: Useful content from Conversation themes or Method
4. **CTA**: What to do next — Crossing language
5. **Sign-off**: Human, warm close — Voice personality

### Subject Line Strategy (World Code Style)
- Pull from Climax tension: "Still [Before State frustration]?"
- Reference Method: "The [Method Name] approach to [topic]"
- Challenge Wrong Belief: "Why [Wrong Belief] is keeping you stuck"
- Story tease: "How I discovered [Method insight]"
- Direct value: "[Conversation theme] tip that [outcome]"

### Tone
- ALWAYS match Voice from `voice.md`
- Follow Hard Rules (non-negotiable)
- Use Authenticity Markers
- First-person and second-person
- Read it out loud—does it sound like the user?

### Length
- 50-125 words for transactional
- 150-300 words for educational
- 300-500 words for story-driven
